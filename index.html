Buckingham Palace

Symbol and home of the British monarchy

That also doubles as an art gallery and a tourist attraction. It is also not only a weekday home of the Queen and Prince Philip, but also the London residence of the Duke of York and the Earl and Countess of Wessex. The palace also houses their offices, as well as those of the Princess Royal and Princess Alexandra, and is the workplace of more than 800 people.

Feel like royalty for a day and be sure to catch the changing of the guards ceremony! Get your tickets here

Tower Bridge

Walk across and admire the London skyline

An iconic London landmark, the approaches to the medieval bridge were marked by the church of St Magnus-the-Martyr on the northern bank and by Southwark Cathedral on the southern shore

Carnaby Street

Want to take a slice of London back with you?

A pedestrianed shopping street in Soho, just a walk away from the more widely known Oxford and Regent's Street. With a mix of fashion and lifestyle retailers as well as a large number of independent fashion boutiques, there's something for everyone!

There's always something going on, no matter the weather, so check out what's happening here

Camden Market

A foodie's heaven

With 1000+ unique shops, stalls, bars and cafes, it is the fourth-most popular visitor attraction in London, attracting approximately 250,000 people each week.
