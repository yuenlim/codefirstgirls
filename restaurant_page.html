Restaurants
London Bridge

nat•ive

Recently relocated from Covent Garden, this accomplished restaurant dishes up inventive cooking with highly seasonal British ingredients.

Bar Douro

An authentic Portuguese restaurant hiding in a railway arch in Flat Iron Square. Bar Douro focuses on shareable small plates, and also boasts a strong tile game, which is important in a world obsessed with aesthetics.

Casa do Frango

The best piri-piri chicken in the city – yeah, I said it Nandos, so what? Also featuring pasteis de nata and a hidden bar, this place is ace


Brixton

Smoke and Salt

Get in a pickle at Smoke ‘n’ Salt, an inventive little restaurant that’s been getting rave reviews. They use old-school preservation methods like fermentation, pickling, drying, smoking and salting with local produce to create powerful, tangy plates of pure flavour.

Bar Alpes

Don’t be a melt, head to Alpes: the permanent spot from the Raclette Brothers dedicated to all things melted cheese. Slather gooey Swiss cheese over hearty Alpine ingredients, or dunk pickles and bread in a bubbling pot of fondue. It’s a fromage sensation.

Duck Duck Goose

Hong Kong meets Brixton at duck duck goose, where Cantonese BBQ rules the roost on a concise and clever menu. Barbecued pork belly, steamed sea bass, wok-fried aubergine and a sharing dish of braised beef short rib are a few stand-out items.

Bars
Shoreditch

Nightjar

Drawing influences from all over the world, Nightjar has a seemingly endless menu of cocktails. The bar aims for stylish sophistication, and hits it pretty much bang on the mark. Swinging jazz nights also help to draw in the cool cats.

Boundary

Open all year round and fully protected from the weather, the popular rooftop of The Boundary is an elevated hot spot the streets of east London. As well as having ideal coverage and heating in the winter, they also adapt the food menu to warm the cockles of your chilly heart. We’re talking hot, gooey fondue and raclette.

Looking Glass Cocktail Club

Another bar that loves a bit of Absinthe, Looking Glass Cocktail Club is Alice in Wonderland-themed and bills itself as a “haven of debauchery”. To enter, you’ll have to step through a looking glass (i.e. a big mirror) and you’re likely to be there until the early hours, cocktail in tow.
